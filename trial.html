Odisha’s Demographic Shift: Aging Population Rises as Youth Base Narrows by 2036

05 June 2025

By 2036, Odisha’s population structure is projected to become more top-heavy, indicating a significant rise in the elderly population. The shrinking youth base raises concerns about future workforce shortages and slower economic momentum. While gender distribution remains largely balanced, subtle age-specific variations are visible, highlighting the need for age- and gender-responsive policies.

05 June 2025

Youth unemployment in urban Odisha peaked sharply at 23.5% in 2017–18, followed by a steady decline to 11.1% in 2023–24. The trend suggests improving job prospects, though rates remain higher than early 2000s levels, signaling the need for sustained policy focus.

05 June 2025

Odisha’s urban population growth rate has dropped significantly from 6.3% in 1961 to a projected 1.0% by 2036. This steady decline signals a maturing urban transition, underscoring the need for renewed strategies in urban planning, service delivery, and rural–urban integration.
