Demography

Despite its status as one of India's poorest states by per capita income and industrial output, Odisha has achieved remarkable success in fertility reduction, with its Total Fertility Rate (TFR) dropping to 1.8 (NFHS-5, 2019-21) – consistently below the national average since 1990. This rapid decline, amidst challenges like high childhood mortality and unfavourable economic conditions, presents a "demographic dilemma" that raises critical questions about future population size and the quality of human resources as the state's age structure shifts. Given the varying stages of demographic transition across Indian states and the absence of a comprehensive census since 2011, this platform is designed to bridge a significant data gap.

As a vital public resource, this platform provides a comprehensive repository of Odisha's demographic data and relevant research publications. Our in-depth analysis of Odisha's demographic transition, leading up to its 2036 millennial milestone, includes key trends, projections, and regional disparities, informing policy across crucial sectors like Health, Education, Employment, Migration, Urbanization, and Population Ageing. By offering accessible, reliable data and cutting-edge insights, it aims to foster a richer and more diverse discourse, encourage evidence-based decision-making, and ultimately contribute to more effective and equitable development strategies throughout the state.

Odisha’s Slowing Population Growth: A Quiet Transition

Odisha’s population grew from 1.46 crore in 1951 to 4.19 crore in 2011, but growth rates have steadily declined. This slowdown reflects a demographic shift marked by lower birth and death rates. Despite this, the state is projected to reach 5.15 crore by 2036, mainly due to increased life expectancy and population momentum

Odisha’s Age Shift: Turning Demographic Change into Opportunity

Odisha is undergoing a major transformation in its population age structure. With a shrinking child and youth population and a growing elderly segment, the state’s working-age group is at its peak, offering a short window for economic gain. This shift demands urgent policy focus to harness the demographic dividend while preparing for rising ageing-related needs in healthcare and social support.

Shifting Balance: The Changing Sex Ratio in Odisha

Once known for its favourable sex ratio, Odisha has witnessed a steady decline over the decades. From a peak of 1,086 females per 1,000 males in 1921 to 979 in 2011, this shift signals emerging gender imbalances shaped by socio-economic and demographic changes.

Falling Fertility, Rising Questions: Odisha at a Demographic Tipping Point

Odisha’s fertility rate has steadily declined, reaching 1.8 in 2021 and projected to fall to 1.48 by 2036. While urban TFR is as low as 1.2, rural areas report 1.9. This shift demands targeted policies for ageing, workforce sustainability, and regional investment in health, education, and skills.
